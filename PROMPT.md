# PROMPT
## BMES Track: Create&Innovate Hackathon @ UH | MAES, AIAA, oSTEM, CougarAI, BMES

In a major accident or disaster, the first 15 minutes are the most critical. Medics currently use the START (Simple Triage and Rapid Treatment) method, but a patient’s condition can change in seconds; a “Yellow” tag (delayed) can quickly become a “Red” tag (immediate) without anyone noticing until it is too late. Furthermore, access to care is often hindered by “Logistical Blindness,” where hospitals lack real-time data on incoming casualties and medics lack visibility into hospital capacity. While Incident Command Systems (ICS) provide a standardized framework for response, the hardware used in the field has yet to catch up. In this track, participants will design an Embedded Triage Ecosystem that automates patient monitoring and uses high-precision data to optimize the flow of casualties from the field to the right care facilities.

This track will challenge participants by:
- Developing technology that improves how information or resources move during a crisis
- Designing hardware that remains effective and reliable in high-pressure or unpredictable environments
- Balancing technical performance with the practical needs of patients and responders in the field.
